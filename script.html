Part 2: The Wide Shot

Long ago, nestled beside mist-draped lochs and cliffs worn by time, there was a village whispered about in old stories.

Its name was Bruhaven, or Gleann Bhru in the older tongue.

A place passed down through fireside tales, where wind carried the scent of heather and hills hummed with secrets. The kind of place that might slip from memory if not spoken aloud often enough.

Bruhaven was not on any map. It lived in memory, where all real magic begins.

Part 4: The Iron Brew

What truly made Bruhaven distinct was the potion that came from its land.

It was bright, orange-gold, and sweet with a strange fizz that danced on the tongue. They called it Iron Brew.

No one really knew where it came from. Some said the soil was enchanted. Others said it was crafted by old alchemists who had long since disappeared into the hills.

It gave strength. It gave laughter. It healed aching limbs and lifted heavy hearts.

To outsiders, it might have looked like a drink. But to Bruhaven, it was tradition. It was ceremony. It was joy in a cup.

The villagers raised it to celebrate. They drank it before a journey. They kept it close and never wasted a drop.

It was their magic.
